Foggy Headlights? Here's How to Restore Them

AK
Arun Kumar Lead Detailer
Apr 20, 2026 4 min read

Why Do Headlights Get Foggy?

If your headlights look cloudy, yellow, or hazy, you're not alone — it's one of the most common issues affecting cars over three years old. Modern headlight lenses are made from polycarbonate plastic, which is lightweight and impact-resistant but vulnerable to UV degradation. Over time, the sun's ultraviolet rays break down the protective clear coat on the lens surface, causing oxidation that makes them appear foggy.

Several factors accelerate this process:

  • UV exposure — the primary cause; cars parked outdoors deteriorate faster
  • Road debris — small rocks and sand create micro-scratches that scatter light
  • Chemical exposure — road salt, cleaning products, and exhaust fumes attack the lens surface
  • Heat from bulbs — internal heat can cause yellowing from the inside out
  • Moisture intrusion — failed seals allow condensation to form inside the housing

The Safety Risk You Can't Ignore

Foggy headlights aren't just a cosmetic issue — they're a serious safety concern. Studies show that oxidized headlights can reduce light output by up to 80%, dramatically decreasing your visibility at night and making your car less visible to other drivers. This is especially dangerous during rain, fog, or on unlit roads where every bit of light matters.

In many regions, severely degraded headlights can even cause your vehicle to fail its roadworthiness inspection. Restoring them is far more cost-effective than replacing the entire headlight assembly, which can cost hundreds of dollars per side.

The Professional Restoration Process

Professional headlight restoration is a multi-step process that brings your lenses back to near-new clarity:

  • Step 1: Masking — surrounding paint and trim are taped off to prevent damage
  • Step 2: Wet sanding — progressive grits (800 to 3000) remove the oxidized layer and scratches
  • Step 3: Polishing — machine polishing with a cutting compound restores optical clarity
  • Step 4: UV sealant application — a professional-grade UV-resistant clear coat is applied to protect against future oxidation

The entire process takes about 30-45 minutes per headlight and the results are immediately dramatic — crystal clear lenses that look factory-fresh.

How Long Does Restoration Last?

With a proper UV sealant, professional headlight restoration typically lasts 2-3 years before any cloudiness returns. The key to longevity is the quality of the UV sealant applied in the final step. Without it, the bare polycarbonate will begin oxidizing again within a few months. At Nyvex, we use a ceramic-infused UV sealant that provides maximum protection and durability.

To extend the life of your restored headlights:

  • Park in shade or a garage when possible
  • Apply a UV protectant spray every few months
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners on the lenses
  • Include headlights in your regular wash routine
